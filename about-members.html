Member Institutions

JLOS Network

JLOS is made up of Eighteen (18) Member Institutions

MOJCA
Ministry of Justice and Constitutional Affairs

The Ministry is responsible for upholding the rule of law, driving forward the legal and justice system, and reforming and safeguarding the Constitution. It facilitates legal advice to Government, drafts legislation, promotes adherence to natural justice, and disseminates legal information to the public.

Judiciary
Judiciary

The Courts of Judicature (Judiciary) comprises the Supreme Court, Court of Appeal, High Court, and Subordinate Courts including Qadhis courts. They administer justice by resolving disputes between individuals and the state, interpreting the Constitution and the Laws of Uganda, and protecting human rights.

Visit Website ↗
MIA
Ministry of Internal Affairs

The Ministry regulates and facilitates exit and entry of nationals, manages immigration documents and work permits, and controls movement of Ugandans and foreign nationals. Through peace-building initiatives, it grants amnesty and reintegrates reporters. The Uganda National Focal Point on Small Arms and Light Weapons (NFP) coordinates control of SALW proliferation.

Visit Website ↗
UPF
Uganda Police Force

The Uganda Police Force secures life and property in partnership with the public in a committed and professional manner to promote development. It protects individual rights, maintains security, public safety and order, and prevents and detects crime across Uganda.

Visit Website ↗
ODPP
Office of the Director of Public Prosecutions

The ODPP provides legal advice relating to the investigation and prosecution of criminal cases. It coordinates and manages prosecution of criminal cases by examining them before registration in court and during prosecutions, ensuring quality and consistency across Uganda's criminal justice system.

Visit Website ↗
UPS
Uganda Prisons Service

The Uganda Prisons Service ensures safe, secure and humane custody with effective rehabilitation of offenders. It administers court-imposed sentences, ensures security of offenders, protects their remaining rights in custody, and ensures they attend trial and are rehabilitated for successful reintegration.

Visit Website ↗
UHRC
Uganda Human Rights Commission

Established under the 1995 Constitution, UHRC investigates human rights violations, visits jails and detention facilities, conducts research and education to enhance respect for human rights, recommends effective measures to Parliament, monitors Government compliance with international human rights treaty obligations, and publishes periodic reports.

Visit Website ↗
JSC
Judicial Service Commission

The Judicial Service Commission recruits competent Judicial Officers and carries out civic education targeting the general public. It also conducts research into the justice system and supervises, monitors and inspects justice dispensation across Uganda.

Visit Website ↗
URSB
Uganda Registration Services Bureau

An autonomous statutory body established in 1998 to take over functions of the Registrar General's Office. URSB carries out all registrations required under relevant laws, maintains registers and records on registrations, evaluates the practicability of relevant laws, conducts research, and charges fees for services.

Visit Website ↗
ULRC
Uganda Law Reform Commission

The Commission constantly studies and reviews Acts and other laws of Uganda with a view to making recommendations for their systematic improvement, development, modernisation through reform and revision. Its aim is to establish an up-to-date and adequate legislative environment for Uganda.

Visit Website ↗
NIRA
National Identification and Registration Authority

Mandated by the Registration of Persons Act 2015, NIRA manages national identification, alien identification, birth registration, death registration, adoption orders registration, replacement of lost/damaged IDs, change of particulars on NIDs, and confirmation of information in the national register.

Visit Website ↗
DCIC
Directorate of Citizenship and Immigration Control

DCIC handles all immigration and citizenship services including issuance of Ugandan Passports, Special Passes, Entry Permits (Work Permits), Dependant Passes, Pupils Passes, Certificates of Residence, granting of Citizenship, and Visas for foreign nationals.

Visit Website ↗
MGLSD
Ministry of Gender, Labour and Social Development (Juvenile Justice)

Within JLOS, the Ministry focuses on juvenile justice — the protection, rehabilitation and reintegration of children and youth in conflict with the law. It oversees remand homes including Naguru and Kampiringisa, providing vocational, psychosocial and rehabilitation support to young offenders.

Visit Website ↗
MoLG
Ministry of Local Government (Local Council Courts)

The Ministry of Local Government oversees the Local Council Court system, which provides community-level dispute resolution across Uganda's districts. Local Council Courts handle minor civil and criminal matters at the grassroots level, serving as an accessible first point of justice for many Ugandans.

Visit Website ↗
ULS
Uganda Law Society

The Uganda Law Society represents the legal profession in Uganda, promoting the rule of law and maintaining professional standards among lawyers. It provides a voice for the legal community on matters of law and justice, and plays a key role in access to justice through legal aid and advocacy.

Visit Website ↗
TAT
Tax Appeals Tribunal

The Tax Appeals Tribunal provides an independent forum for taxpayers to appeal against tax assessments and decisions made by the Uganda Revenue Authority. It ensures fair and impartial resolution of tax disputes, contributing to a sound legal framework for investment and commercial activity.

Visit Website ↗
LDC
Law Development Centre

The Law Development Centre provides legal training and continuing legal education in Uganda. It offers the Bar Course for admission to the legal profession and professional development programmes for practising lawyers, contributing to the quality and capacity of Uganda's justice sector workforce.

Visit Website ↗
CADER
Centre for Arbitration and Dispute Resolution

CADER promotes and administers alternative dispute resolution in Uganda, including arbitration, mediation and conciliation. It provides a cost-effective alternative to court litigation for commercial and civil disputes, contributing to a more efficient and accessible justice system for businesses and citizens.
