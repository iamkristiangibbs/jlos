About JLOS

What is the Justice Law and Order Sector (JLOS)?

The Justice Law and Order Sector (JLOS) is a sector-wide approach that brings together 18 institutions with closely linked mandates responsible for administering justice, maintaining law and order and promoting the observance of human rights.

JLOS is a major player in the promotion of the rule of law in Uganda. Through a sector-wide approach (SWAp), the sector brings together state and non-state actors who play complementary roles in planning, budgeting, programme implementation, monitoring and evaluation.

It is an innovative approach of the Government of Uganda built around the 3 Cs — Communication, Coordination and Cooperation — among several stakeholders.

Our Purpose

Mission

To improve the safety of the person, security of property, and access to justice for inclusive growth with the overall goal to "promote the rule of law."

Vision

To ensure that people in Uganda live in a safe and just society.

Structure

How Does JLOS Function?

The sector is centered on joint planning through committees and working groups at national and sub-national levels. The Secretariat is only a coordination unit — we are all JLOS.

National Level Committees

Leadership Committee

Comprised of all heads of institutions, ultimately accountable for JLOS services. Responsible for political leadership, guidance and articulating the JLOS Vision.

Steering Committee

Responsible for policy formulation, coordination, fundraising, external accountability, and quality assurance of JLOS results to the people of Uganda.

Technical Committee

Comprises technical personnel from JLOS institutions headed by departmental heads. Primarily operates through specialist working groups.

Working Groups

Working groups serve as extensions of the Technical Committee, providing in-depth consideration of reform areas. They report to the Technical Committee for decisions on resource allocation.
