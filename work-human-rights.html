Human Rights

Programme Area

Upholding Rights, Empowering Citizens

Human rights observance is a critical feature of Uganda's governance and the rule of law, as emphasised in Vision 2040. The Sector is focusing on strengthening JLOS institutions to eliminate human rights violations as well as promoting citizens' rights and obligations within the framework of the National Action Plan for Human Rights.

Human rights violations and abuses are partly caused by a lack of information among citizens about their rights and where to seek protection. JLOS has commenced the development of a coherent framework that would streamline and further guide knowledge empowerment of the people of Uganda.

Strategy

Key Focus Areas

📚
Civic Education & Rights Awareness

Developing a coherent civic education strategy to empower Ugandans with knowledge about their rights and obligations, support compliance with the law, provide platforms for social accountability, and reinforce participatory governance.

🏛️
Institutional Strengthening

Strengthening JLOS institutions — in particular the Uganda Human Rights Commission and Judicial Service Commission — to investigate violations, adjudicate complaints, and monitor state compliance with national and international human rights obligations.

🤝
National Action Plan for Human Rights

Implementing Uganda's National Action Plan for Human Rights, mainstreaming gender, children's rights, and disability into JLOS programmes, and combating sexual and gender-based violence through dedicated institutions and community-level interventions.

UHRC & JSC

Civic Education Strategy

The Uganda Human Rights Commission (UHRC) and the Judicial Service Commission (JSC) have commenced the development of a joint civic education strategy. Among its objectives, the strategy will support compliance with legal and human rights standards, provide platforms for social accountability, reinforce participatory governance, and foster constitutionalism and the rule of law.

This initiative recognises that informed citizens are the foundation of a rights-respecting society, and that sustainable human rights improvements depend on communities understanding and asserting the protections available to them.
