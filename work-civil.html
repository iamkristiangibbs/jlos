Programme Area

Commercial Justice & an Enabling Environment

The Sector continues to facilitate an enabling environment for productivity, investment, and competitiveness by enhancing efficiency in settling commercial disputes through supporting an efficient legal framework, thereby reducing the ease of doing business. In the Doing Business Report 2020, Uganda registered an improvement in the ease of doing business index to 60 from 56.94 — ranking highest in the EAC region.

The rule of law regulates economic activity, defines and affirms rights and obligations, and clarifies to investors the laws and institutional environment for doing business. An efficient and effective justice delivery system is fundamental for poverty reduction and inclusive growth. JLOS works to ensure that civil and commercial laws are accessible, enforceable, and responsive to Uganda's development needs.

Strategy

Key Focus Areas

📜
Access to Commercial Laws

Reviewing and reforming critical legislation — including the Land Valuation Law and the UNRA Act — to reduce the complexity of commercial rules and procedures, improve law accessibility, and promote business education across Uganda.

🗂️
Business Registries

Enhancing the efficiency of all business registries through integration and automation. The Electronic Chattels Registry (Secured Moveable Properties Registry) is now fully operational, and the average time to register a business has been reduced to 3 hours.

⚖️
Dispute Resolution

Strengthening the Commercial Court and specialist courts as centres for excellence in dispute resolution, expanding mediation, minor claims procedures, and ADR, while reviewing rules and guidelines that delay case resolution.

Context

Challenges & Ongoing Priorities

01
Inaccessibility of Commercial Laws

The complexity of rules and procedures in commercial justice, combined with non-enforcement of commercial laws, continues to constrain Uganda's business environment and deter investment.

02
Ill-Equipped Registries

Many business registries still rely on manual work processes. Limited staff capacity in e-registry systems and inadequate stakeholder awareness of the registration process remain significant barriers.

03
Limited ADR Rollout

The rollout of mediation, minor claims procedures, and other alternative dispute resolution mechanisms remains limited outside Kampala, reducing access to affordable commercial dispute resolution for businesses upcountry.

04
Case Management Automation

The legal framework efficiency index is expected to improve as automation levels increase in the Case Management System. Continued investment in digital systems is required to sustain and accelerate this progress.
