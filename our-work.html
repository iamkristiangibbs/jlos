Focus Areas

Core Programme Areas

JLOS works across five interconnected areas to deliver a justice system that is accessible, efficient, and equitable for all Ugandans.

Criminal Justice
Programme Area

Criminal Justice

JLOS works to ensure that criminal justice is delivered swiftly, fairly, and humanely across Uganda. This includes reducing case backlogs, improving prosecution quality, protecting victims' rights, and ensuring that prisons are humane and rehabilitative.

01

Reducing case backlog in all tiers of the Judiciary through fast-track courts and plea bargaining.

02

Strengthening prosecution quality through ODPP training and case management systems.

03

Improving prison conditions and expanding rehabilitation programmes for offenders.

Civil Justice
Programme Area

Civil Justice

Civil justice reforms focus on making the resolution of civil disputes — including land, family, commercial, and labour matters — more accessible and affordable, especially for vulnerable populations.

01

Expanding legal aid through the JLOS Legal Aid Programme and justice centres.

02

Promoting Alternative Dispute Resolution through CADER and local council courts.

03

Digitising civil court records for faster, more transparent dispute resolution.

Human Rights
Programme Area

Human Rights

JLOS works to embed human rights principles across all member institutions, ensuring that the justice system protects rather than violates the rights of citizens, including the most marginalised.

01

Supporting UHRC to investigate and adjudicate human rights violation cases.

02

Mainstreaming gender, children's rights, and disability across sector institutions.

03

Combating SGBV through dedicated special sessions and survivor support services.

Explore Human Rights →
Transitional Justice
Programme Area

Transitional Justice

Uganda's transitional justice framework addresses legacy issues from past conflicts, particularly in Northern Uganda. JLOS coordinates mechanisms for truth-telling, accountability, and reconciliation.

01

Documenting atrocities and supporting survivor testimonies through the ADC project.

02

Facilitating community reconciliation through traditional and formal justice mechanisms.

03

Supporting the Amnesty Commission to reintegrate former combatants.

Explore Transitional Justice →
Accountability
Programme Area

Accountability & Governance

JLOS strengthens institutional accountability by promoting transparency, combating corruption, and ensuring that justice institutions are governed in line with constitutional principles and citizens' expectations.

01

Supporting anti-corruption mechanisms within JLOS institutions through internal oversight.

02

Promoting e-justice and digital systems to reduce discretion and opportunity for corruption.

03

Publishing annual performance reports for all member institutions.
