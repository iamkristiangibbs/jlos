Criminal Justice

Programme Area

Preventing Crime, Delivering Justice

The capacity to prevent and respond to crime is essential in ensuring law and order in the country. Key among the critical components is strengthening the capacity of crime-fighting agencies — including investigation, prosecution, and correctional institutions — to reduce the crime rate and effectively and efficiently respond to crime.

In addition, the Sector implements specific measures to boost the rehabilitation of offenders, including juveniles, with the aim of reducing recidivism levels. As a result of investments into crime-fighting agencies, the conviction rate increased to 63%, and investments in the rehabilitation of inmates led to a reduction in reoffending rates from 25% in 2015 to 15% in 2019.

Strategy

Key Focus Areas

🔎
Crime Prevention & Intelligence

Implementing a comprehensive crime prevention strategy through increased use of crime intelligence, community policing, and evidence-based approaches to reducing criminal activity across Uganda.

🧪
Forensic Science & Technology

Increasing the use of forensic science and integrating policing case management systems within UPF with other criminal justice institutions — including ODPP, Judiciary, DGAL, and Uganda Prisons Service — to streamline crime records and data management.

♻️
Offender Rehabilitation

Strengthening rehabilitation programmes for offenders — including juveniles — while increasing UPS holding capacity to address prison overcrowding and reduce reoffending rates through targeted vocational and psychosocial support.

Context

Challenges & Ongoing Priorities

Despite significant progress, the criminal justice sub-programme continues to address systemic challenges that limit the sector's ability to deliver swift, fair, and humane justice.

01
Prison Overcrowding

The daily average prison population has risen from 30,509 in FY2010/11 to over 60,000 in 2019 — an annual growth of 8.3% compared to the 3.0% national population growth — resulting in serious overcrowding that strains rehabilitation efforts.

02
Gaps in Investigation & Prosecution Standards

Weaknesses persist in crime-fighting agencies, including the absence of comprehensive standards for investigation, prosecution, and correctional services, and the limited use of scientific evidence in crime management.

03
Staffing Deficits

Limited staffing levels in the Uganda Police Force and Uganda Prisons Service continue to affect the capacity to prevent crime, manage custodial facilities, and deliver quality rehabilitation outcomes.

04
Fragmented Information Systems

Case management systems across criminal justice institutions — UPF, ODPP, Judiciary, and UPS — remain poorly integrated, limiting the sector's ability to track cases efficiently from arrest through to sentencing and rehabilitation.
