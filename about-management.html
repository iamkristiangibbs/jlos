JLOS Committee Architecture

The sector collaboration architecture is built on a committee system at both national and sub-national levels. The Secretariat is a coordination unit — we are all JLOS.

JLOS Leadership Committee

This is the Committee comprised of all heads of institutions, who are ultimately accountable for the delivery of JLOS services in the country. It is responsible for political leadership and guidance to the Sector and for articulating the JLOS Vision and Sector development policy.

JLOS Steering Committee

The Steering Committee is the body responsible for policy formulation, coordination, fundraising, external accountability, and quality assurance of JLOS results to the people of Uganda.

JLOS Technical Committee

The Technical Committee comprises technical personnel from JLOS institutions, headed by departmental heads. It primarily operates through working groups and reports to the Steering Committee on sector-wide technical matters.

Working Groups

Due to the breadth of the reform programme, working groups serve as extensions of the Technical Committee, providing in-depth consideration that would not be possible within the Technical Committee itself.

Sub-national Structures

Regional Chain-linked Committees (RCCs)

Regional-level coordination bodies that oversee JLOS implementation across districts within each region.

District Chain-Linked Committees (DCCs)

District-level bodies coordinating delivery of JLOS services at the local level across Uganda's 146 districts.
