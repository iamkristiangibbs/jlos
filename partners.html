Our Partners

JLOS works with a range of development partners who provide technical and financial support to the sector's reform agenda.

EU
Bilateral

European Union

The EU is one of JLOS's primary development partners, providing budget support and technical assistance for justice sector reform.

UNDP
Multilateral

UNDP Uganda

United Nations Development Programme supports JLOS on governance, rule of law, and human rights programming.

WB
Multilateral

World Bank

The World Bank supports infrastructure, judicial reform, and access to justice initiatives within the JLOS framework.

GIZ
Bilateral — Germany

GIZ / Federal Germany

The German development agency provides technical expertise on justice reform, institutional strengthening, and training.

USAID
Bilateral — USA

USAID

USAID supports JLOS programmes on legal aid, counter-corruption, transitional justice, and citizen security.

SIDA
Bilateral — Sweden

Sida / Sweden

The Swedish International Development Cooperation Agency supports human rights, gender justice, and democratic governance.

IRL
Bilateral — Ireland

Irish Aid

Irish Aid supports access to justice, legal aid, and community-level dispute resolution under the JLOS framework.

DAN
Bilateral — Denmark

DANIDA

The Danish development cooperation supports anti-corruption, human rights, and governance reform programmes.

NL
Bilateral — Netherlands

Netherlands Embassy

Supports rule of law, access to justice, and SGBV prevention and response under the JLOS sector programme.
